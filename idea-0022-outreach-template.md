# idea-0022 — Pilot Customer Outreach Templates
*Version: 0.1 — 2026-03-28*

---

## Target Persona

**Title:** Plant Manager, Operations Manager, Warehouse Operations Manager, Director of Manufacturing Operations, Fulfillment Center Manager

**Company size:** 50–500 floor workers. Owner-operated or regional division of a mid-market manufacturer. Has authority to run a pilot without enterprise procurement approval.

**Industry sub-verticals to prioritize (ranked):**

1. **3PL / fulfillment warehouses** — highest density of floor workers doing repetitive scan-and-log tasks; ops managers are margin-obsessed and tool-receptive; easy to reach on LinkedIn
2. **Food & beverage manufacturing** — gloved hands are mandatory (food safety), high defect-log burden, strong interest in reducing contamination incident paperwork
3. **Automotive parts / tier-2 suppliers** — strict quality logging requirements (IATF 16949), defect tracking is compliance-driven, high willingness to pay for accuracy tools
4. **Medical device / pharma manufacturing** — highest data accuracy need, but longer sales cycles and regulatory caution; approach later once you have a reference customer

**Where to find them:**

- LinkedIn search strings:
  - `"operations manager" "manufacturing" "warehouse" "50 employees" OR "100 employees"`
  - `"plant manager" "3PL" OR "fulfillment" "North America"`
  - `"director of operations" "food manufacturing" OR "food processing"`
- Trade associations: MHI (Material Handling Institute), APICS/ASCM, NFPA, AME (Association for Manufacturing Excellence)
- Trade shows: ProMat (Chicago, March), MODEX (Atlanta, March alt years), Pack Expo, IMTS
- LinkedIn groups: "Manufacturing Operations Network", "Warehouse & Logistics Professionals"

## Template 1 — Cold LinkedIn Message

**Character limit: under 300 characters. Lead with their problem. One ask.**

---

Hey [Name] — plant managers I talk to say the biggest invisible tax on their floor is manual data entry with gloved hands. Building voice control for exactly that. Would a 20-min call be worth it? Happy to share what we're seeing.

---

**Notes on delivery:**
- Personalize the opening line if you can reference something specific — their company's product, a recent hire, a LinkedIn post about floor efficiency
- Do not mention "AI" or "machine learning" — it signals vaporware to this persona
- Send connection request with the message attached, not after
- Best days: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am or 12–1pm local time for ops managers

## Template 2 — Cold Email

**Subject:** Voice control for your floor workers — looking for one design partner

---

Hi [Name],

I'm building a voice control system for factory and warehouse workers — specifically for situations where both hands are occupied and gloved.

The problem I keep hearing: workers have to stop what they're doing to log a unit, flag a defect, or call a supervisor. That interruption happens dozens of times per shift, per worker. The data entry lag is real, and the workflow break is real.

What I've built: a push-to-activate voice system that lets workers log production data, flag quality issues, and alert supervisors by speaking a short phrase — without touching anything. It runs locally, so production data stays on your network.

I'm looking for one operations leader to run a free 60-day pilot. You'd get early access, direct input into what gets built next, and a system configured specifically for your workflow.

The ask is small: a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit.

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Phone or calendar link]

---

**Notes on delivery:**
- Subject line is direct and functional — no clickbait. Works better with this persona than clever subject lines.
- Keep the body as-is. Do not add a feature list or pricing.
- If you have a referral or shared connection, add one sentence before the last paragraph: "I got your name from [X] — hope that's okay."
- Follow up once at day 5 if no reply. One follow-up only. Short: "Did this land in a bad week? Happy to try again later."
