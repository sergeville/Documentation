# PRD — Product Requirements Document

A PRD defines *what* a product must do (not *how* to build it). It is the contract between product thinking and engineering.

## Sections

- **Problem statement** — what pain exists and for whom
- **Goals & success metrics** — how you'll know it worked
- **User personas** — who uses it
- **Functional requirements** — what the product must do (features, behaviors)
- **Non-functional requirements** — performance, security, scalability constraints
- **Out of scope** — explicit exclusions to prevent scope creep
- **Assumptions & dependencies** — what must be true for this to work

## BMAD Pipeline Position

```
Product Brief  →  PRD  →  Architecture  →  Epics  →  Stories  →  Dev
(why/who)        (what)    (how)           (chunks)   (tasks)
```

The **product brief** is lighter — captures the vision, problem, and direction.
The **PRD** comes after and goes deeper into requirements.
