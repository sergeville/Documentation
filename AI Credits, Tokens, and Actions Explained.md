**AI Credits vs. Tokens: Conversion Summary**

## **Overview**

AI platforms use **Tokens** as the technical unit of measurement (roughly 0.75 words), while they use **Credits** as a simplified billing unit to cover high-compute **Discrete Actions** like generating images or analyzing video.

## **Token & Credit Comparison Table**

This table illustrates how **1,000 Credits** (assuming a standard value of \~$10.00) translate into different types of AI usage.

| Action Type | Discrete Action Unit | Token Equivalent | 1,000 Credits Can Buy... |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Simple Text** | 1,000 Words | \~1,333 Tokens | \~3,000,000+ Tokens |
| **Image Generation** | 1 Image (1024x1024) | \~3,000 Tokens | \~300 Images |
| **Video Analysis** | 1 Minute of Video | \~15,780 Tokens | \~190 Minutes of Video |
| **Audio Analysis** | 1 Minute of Audio | \~1,920 Tokens | \~1,500 Minutes of Audio |
| **Reasoning/Thinking** | 1 Deep Query | \~50,000 Tokens | \~60 Deep Reasoning Tasks |
| **Web Search** | 1 Search Action | \~5,000 Tokens | \~600 Research Tasks |

## ---

**Key Takeaways**

* **Predictability:** Credits protect the user from "token spikes." For example, a credit-based system might charge you **1 credit** to summarize a PDF whether it is 5 pages or 50 pages.  
* **Multimodal Cost:** Visual actions (Video/Image) are significantly more "token-heavy" than text, which is why platforms often gate them behind a credit system rather than a flat token count.  
* **Background Usage:** "Discrete actions" often include hidden tokens for system instructions, search results, and "chain-of-thought" reasoning that the user never sees in the final output.
